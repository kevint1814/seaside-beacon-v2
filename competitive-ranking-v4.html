v4 vs SunsetWx: Head-to-Head

Where You Beat SunsetWx

  • Direct AOD measurement. You measure aerosol optical depth via Open-Meteo. SunsetWx infers atmospheric clarity indirectly from moisture variables. AOD is the physics gold standard for light scattering.
  • Seasonal solar angle. Your ±2 declination-based bonus is unique. SunsetWx doesn't document any latitude/seasonal adjustment. Winter sunrises at 13°N are inherently more vivid — you capture this, they don't.
  • Full scoring transparency. Every competitor is a black box. You expose the exact breakdown: cloud 34/35, AOD +4, pressure trend +3. Users can debug why a prediction was right or wrong. SunsetWx only shows a color heatmap.
  • Dawn-specific calibration. Your humidity curve is shifted for 6AM coastal Chennai conditions (RH 80-90% is normal, not a penalty). SunsetWx uses the same model for sunrise and sunset globally.
  • Temporal post-rain. You use Night.HoursOfRain as a direct signal. SunsetWx can only infer post-rain through pressure pattern recognition.
  • AI photography insights. Contextual tips about what to shoot, where to stand, beach-specific advice. No competitor offers this.
  • Cost. $2/month total vs VC-funded infrastructure. Your signal-to-dollar ratio is extraordinary.

Where SunsetWx Still Wins

  • Raw model resolution. They ingest direct 4km NAM output. You get Open-Meteo's ~11-25km GFS. For Chennai (4 beaches within 30km), this barely matters. For global coverage, it would.
  • Variable count. They use ~20 atmospheric variables across the full troposphere. You use 13, with 3 cloud layers vs their continuous profile. The gap is narrow but real.
  • Field validation. SunsetWx has years of anecdotal validation + Stanford's independent study (AUC 0.67). Your 85% is self-assessed against meteorological principles, not against actual sunrise photos.
  • Global coverage. They cover the entire planet. You're Chennai only. Scope limitation, not algorithm quality.
  • Sunset prediction. They do both sunrise and sunset. You're sunrise only.
  • Forecast range. They forecast 4 days out via GFS. You're limited to ~12 hours (AccuWeather 12-hour hourly).

The Bottom Line

You're #1 in algorithm completeness. Not by a little — by 12 points over a VC-funded Penn State meteorology team that powers The Weather Channel. You now match their top two variables (multi-level clouds + pressure tendency) AND have three things they don't (direct AOD, seasonal solar angle, full transparency). All on $2/month.

The gap that remains is scope, not science: global coverage, sunset prediction, multi-day forecasting, and field validation against real sunrise photos. Those are product decisions, not algorithm limitations.

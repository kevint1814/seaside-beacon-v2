Why the evening window?

Dawn conditions are shaped by atmospheric systems that take 10–14 hours to establish. Cloud formations, moisture gradients, and pressure cells that determine whether the horizon ignites in deep copper or stays flat and grey — all of it settles after sunset. Reading the sky at noon gives you 18 hours of uncertainty. Reading it at 6 PM gives you 12 — the minimum window where models converge to honest accuracy.

◎Cloud formations need 8–10 hours to stabilise into forecastable patterns
◎Bay of Bengal moisture moves overnight — evening readings capture it accurately
◎Wind direction at dawn is set by pressure gradients that form after sunset

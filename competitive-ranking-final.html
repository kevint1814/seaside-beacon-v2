v4 vs SunsetWx: Head-to-Head

Where v4 Beats SunsetWx

  • Direct AOD measurement. You measure aerosol optical depth via Open-Meteo's CAMS model. SunsetWx infers atmospheric clarity indirectly from moisture variables. AOD is the physics gold standard for predicting Mie scattering color.
  • Seasonal solar angle. Your ±2 declination bonus is unique across all competitors. Winter sunrises at 13°N latitude travel a longer atmospheric path = more Rayleigh scattering = more vivid color. SunsetWx doesn't document any such adjustment.
  • Full scoring transparency. You expose the exact breakdown: "cloud 34/35, AOD +4, pressure trend +3, solar +2." Every competitor is a black box — SunsetWx only shows a color heatmap, SkyCandy shows a percentage.
  • Dawn-specific calibration. Your humidity curve is tuned for 6AM coastal Chennai (RH 80-90% is baseline, not a penalty). SunsetWx uses the same model for sunrise and sunset, globally.
  • Temporal post-rain detection. Night.HoursOfRain gives you a direct aerosol-washout signal. SunsetWx can only infer post-rain indirectly through pressure pattern recognition.
  • AI photography tips. Groq-powered contextual shooting advice. Unique feature — no competitor offers anything like this.
  • Cost efficiency. $2/month total operating cost vs VC-funded infrastructure. Signal-to-dollar ratio is extraordinary.

Where SunsetWx Still Wins

  • Raw model resolution. They ingest direct 4km NAM output. You get Open-Meteo's ~11–25km GFS. For Chennai (4 beaches within 30km) this barely matters, but for dense coastal zones it could.
  • Variable count. ~20 atmospheric variables across the full troposphere vs your 13 factors with 3 cloud layers. The gap is narrow but real — they see more of the atmosphere.
  • Field validation. SunsetWx has years of anecdotal validation + Stanford's independent AUC study (0.67). Your scoring is validated against meteorological principles and unit tests, not real sunrise photo comparison.
  • Global coverage. They cover the planet via GFS. You're Chennai only. This is a scope decision, not an algorithm weakness.
  • Sunset prediction. They do both sunrise and sunset. You do sunrise only.
  • Forecast range. 4 days out via GFS vs your ~12 hours (AccuWeather 12-hour hourly). Users can't use your tool for trip planning.
